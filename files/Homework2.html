Introduction

Consumer Price index is an important indicator of economy to the general public. In a developing country like Turkey, even the smallest issues may cause the prices to rise immediately. Over the last couple of years, and especially this year with Covid-19 causing shutdowns and lockdowns, we experienced steep increases of prices in the market in Turkey. The high positive change in prices determine the inflation rate. Inflation rate does not include anything other than the change of prices in the market so even if the inflation rate is very high, we cannot say anything about the purchasing power of the people. However, in Turkey annual interest rate for salaries or other income does not get close to inflation. Change in prices heavily effect people in Turkey and their purchasing power. Consumer Price index is deeply connected to different factors. In this study we will explore the correlation of Consumer Price Index to Industrial Production Index, Employment and Exchange Rates.

  • Prices in the market are related to production rate of the country. High production increases the product in the market and that causes the prices to drop. With this model we can make the assumption that we will discover a negative correlation between price and production. We will be looking at Industrial Production Index(IPI) to see changes in production rate through the years. Later we will look for correlation between this measure and our main subject: Consumer Price Index.

  • Production relies on employment. If the employment increases then the production will follow. To make an assumption for the correlation between Consumer Price Index and employment we do as we did about Industrial Production Index. The logical assumption would be that employment and Consumer Price Index are negatively correlated.

  • Turkey is a developing economy therefore it has to operate with other currencies to carry out transactions with foreign markets. These currencies are generally US Dollar and Euro. Being the currencies of giant economies of US and Europe they are more valuable that the modest Turkish Lira. So when we exchange our money for dollar the money amount decreases. Political turmoil generally increases the exchange rates and that is what has been happening in recent years. When our purchases are outsourced, exchange rate determines the money we pay. And Turkey imports many goods. It is safe to assume that an increase in exchange rate will also increase Consumer Price Index. In this study we will be analyzing dollar/TL.

Correlation Between Consumer Price Index and Industrial Production Index

Our assumption on these two measures was that there would be a negative correlation. But we observed both Consumer Price Index and Industrial Production Index increased over 2011-2019 period. Using Scatter Plot and Correlation Matrix we came up to a not so significant correlation. Now let us do the test and chech if our assumption was right.

## 
##  Pearson's product-moment correlation
## 
## data:  production_data$CPI and production_data$IPI
## t = 13.617, df = 106, p-value < 2.2e-16
## alternative hypothesis: true correlation is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  0.7167255 0.8573947
## sample estimates:
##       cor 
## 0.7976621

We can say that with p-value being very small, we can reject null hypothesis. This result tells us there seems to be a significant amount of correlation between CPI and Industrial Production Index.
